## PART 1 — LEAD GENERATION (WhatsApp DM Marketing)

### 1.1 — Strategic Intent

WhatsApp DM is the primary acquisition channel for clients 1–10. The reason is not that it's the best channel long-term — it's that it's the fastest to test, the easiest to personalize, and it reaches hotel GMs where they already spend most of their working day.

The goal of lead gen is not to sell. The goal is to generate a qualified conversation. A qualified conversation is one where: (a) the person has the after-hours inquiry problem, and (b) they are the decision maker or can get us in front of one.

Everything else — nurture, content, LinkedIn — is a supporting channel. It warms leads. It builds credibility. It makes the cold DM slightly less cold. But it does not replace the DM.

---

### 1.2 — ICP Scoring: Who to Contact

Before adding any property to your outreach queue, score them. Minimum score: 7/10 to enter Tier 1, 5/10 for Tier 2.

**ICP Scorecard (10 points max):**

| Criterion | Points | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Has WhatsApp publicly listed on website or Google | 2 | Hard requirement — if no WhatsApp, deprioritize |
| 3★ or 4★ star rating | 1 | 5★ chains have enterprise contracts; 1-2★ too price-sensitive |
| 40–200 rooms | 1 | Sweet spot: feels the pain but has budget |
| Independent or small regional chain (<5 properties) | 1 | Decision-maker is accessible |
| Located in Klang Valley / KL for Phase 1 (or JB, Penang as stretch) | 1 | Geographic concentration for referrals |
| ADR RM 150–450 (visible from Booking.com/Agoda) | 1 | Enough ADR for recovery ROI to be obvious |
| Named GM/Revenue Manager found (LinkedIn, website, Google) | 1 | Personalization increases reply rate significantly |
| TripAdvisor or Google review mentioning slow response / missed booking | 1 | Pain signal — quote in the DM |
| No "24-hour front desk" listing or no overnight staff (reviews or website) | 1 | Confirms the problem exists |

**Tier Assignment:**
- Score 8–10: Tier 1 — contact this week (Priority: WhatsApp > Call + Email)
- Score 5–7: Tier 2 — contact after Tier 1 exhausted
- Score <5: Do not contact in Phase 1

**Existing lead list:** See `docs/sales_playbook.md` — 48 properties already pre-qualified. Work this list before sourcing new leads.

### 1.3 — List Building: How to Find New Leads

When the 48-property list is exhausted or needs refreshing:

**Source 1: Google Maps**
Search: `"hotel" + "KL / Petaling Jaya / Shah Alam / Cheras"` in Google Maps. Filter: 3–4 star. Open each listing and check: (a) is there a WhatsApp number? (b) is there a website with a named contact?

**Source 2: Booking.com / Agoda**
Search by city, filter by 3–4 star. Note hotel name, room count (shown in listing), and ADR. Cross-reference with Google Maps for WhatsApp.

**Source 3: TripAdvisor**
Search by city, sort by Hotels. Reviews mentioning "no one answered my message" or "had to wait until morning" are pure pain signals — screenshot and use in the DM.

**Source 4: LinkedIn**
Search: `"General Manager" + "hotel" + "Kuala Lumpur"` in LinkedIn People search. This gives names with pictures — much higher reply rate when you address them by name in the DM.

**Source 5: MOTAC Registry**
Malaysian Tourism, Arts and Culture Ministry hotel registry. Publicly available. Lists registered hotels by state, star rating, and room count. Direct link via official MOTAC website.

### 1.5 — The 4-Touch Sequence (Full Campaign Per Lead)

Every lead gets a maximum of 4 touches before being marked inactive (not "dead" — just inactive; re-contact is appropriate in 60–90 days if market conditions change or you have new proof).

**TOUCH 1 — Day 0: The Opener**

Use the appropriate opener from `sales_script_complete.md` based on the lead's profile. Personalization variables to always include: `[Hotel Name]`, `[GM Name if known]`, specific detail from research (review quote, listing observation, or number of rooms).

Decision tree:
```
Named GM found on LinkedIn? → Use their first name
WhatsApp number listed publicly? → Use Opener A or C
No WhatsApp listed? → Use email instead of WhatsApp, adjust copy
TripAdvisor review mentioning slow response? → Quote it (anonymously)
Known ADR from Booking.com? → Use Opener B with calculated number
Warm referral / existing relationship? → Use Opener D, mention referrer
```

**Best send times:** Tuesday–Thursday, 9:00–11:00am. Avoid Monday mornings (operational catch-up time), Friday afternoons, and weekends. Hotel GMs check WhatsApp constantly during the weekday morning.

get many WhatsApp inquiries after your front desk wraps up for the night?

(Happy to skip straight to the numbers if that's more useful)

Thanks

### 1.6 — The Qualification Conversation

Once a lead replies, your one goal is to establish three things before booking a call:

1. **Do they have the problem?** (after-hours inquiry volume > 0)
2. **Are they the decision maker?** (or can they get you to one)
3. **Are they open to exploring a solution?** (not just being polite)

**Qualification message flow** (after any opener gets a response):

Step 1 — Confirm the pain:
```
Great — and just to understand your situation better:
when guests send WhatsApp messages to [Hotel Name] after reception
closes, what typically happens? Do they wait until morning,
or is someone monitoring?
```

Step 2 — Get a volume estimate:
```
And on a busy week — school holidays, weekends — how many messages
would you say come in after 10pm?
(Even a rough guess works)
```

Step 3 — Confirm decision authority:
```
Are you the person who'd make a call on something like this,
or would there be someone else to loop in?
```

Step 4 — Book the call:
```
Based on what you've described — [their situation paraphrased] —
it sounds like there's a real opportunity here.

I can show you the AI demo and run your specific numbers in about
20 minutes on Zoom. Would [Tuesday 2pm] or [Wednesday 4pm] work?
```

**Qualification disqualifiers — move to inactive if:**
- They confirm 24/7 staffed reservations (no problem to solve)
- They say they've just signed a long-term contract with a competitor
- They are clearly not the decision maker and refuse to introduce you to one
- Response is dismissive or hostile (don't waste sequences on them)

### 1.7 — Parallel Channel: LinkedIn as Credibility Warmer

LinkedIn does not replace the DM. It makes the DM warmer. A GM who has seen your posts twice before your DM lands is 2–3× more likely to reply.

**Minimum viable LinkedIn engine (2 posts per week):**

| Week | Post 1 | Post 2 |
|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | "8 hotel GMs told me the same thing about WhatsApp..." (the insight post) | The revenue math (specific numbers, no product mention) |
| 2 | "We just went live at our first KL hotel — here's what happened in week 1" | Screenshot of an AI conversation (anonymized) + the response time stat |
| 3 | "What guests actually ask Malaysian hotel AIs at 2am" (real inquiry types) | The guarantee post: "Why we offer 30 days free" |
| 4 | "Hotel GM called me last week to say the AI captured 3 bookings while he was asleep" | The daily report screenshot + "what your GM sees every morning" |

**Rules for LinkedIn posts:**
- Always write as a person, never as a company
- Lead with a specific number or unexpected observation (not "I'm excited to share...")
- End with a question that invites GMs to comment (increases reach)
- Tag hotel properties or GMs only when they've given permission
- Never post the same day as a mass DM campaign (space them apart)

**The DM-LinkedIn connection:**
Before sending a DM to a named GM, connect with them on LinkedIn first with a no-pitch connection request:
```
Hi [Name] — I work with hotels in KL on WhatsApp inquiry capture.
Thought it'd be good to connect.
```
If they accept and view your posts before the DM lands: warm lead. If they don't accept: send the DM anyway — but now you have their attention twice.

### 1.8 — What NOT to Do in Lead Gen

These are common mistakes that kill reply rates and burn lists:

- **Do not send the same opener to everyone.** GMs talk to each other. Copy-paste DMs get screenshotted and shared in WhatsApp groups.
- **Do not pitch in the first message.** The only job of Touch 1 is to get a reply.
- **Do not DM and email simultaneously.** Pick one per person. WhatsApp first; email as fallback if no WhatsApp listed.
- **Do not follow up every day.** The Day 3 / Day 7 / Day 14 rhythm is designed. Faster follow-ups feel desperate.
- **Do not mention price in the DM sequence.** Price is revealed on the call, after you've anchored the value.
- **Do not use a business WhatsApp account for outreach.** It reads as corporate and gets ignored. Use your personal number. Hotel GMs respond to people, not brands.
- **Do not DM Tier 3 regional chains the same way you DM independent GMs.** Chain properties require a different entry point — ask for the Group GM or Head of Operations, not the property-level GM.

## PART 2 — LEAD NURTURE

### 2.1 — What Nurture Is and Isn't

Nurture is for leads who have responded but are not ready to book a call. It is NOT for:
- Leads who haven't replied at all (that's the DM sequence)
- Leads who said "not interested" (close them out)
- Leads who booked a call (that's Sales)

A lead enters nurture when they engage — reply with questions, express interest but say "not now," or ask for more info — but haven't committed to a call.

Nurture has one goal: **stay top of mind as a credible, helpful expert until they're ready to move.**

---

### 2.2 — Lead Classification on Entry

When a lead replies to your DM, classify them immediately:

| Signal | Classification | Action |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| "Yes we have the problem, let's talk" | Hot — Sales | Book call immediately |
| "Interesting, tell me more" | Warm — Nurture Sequence A | Send value, soft CTA |
| "Not now, maybe later" | Cool — Nurture Sequence B | Low-touch monthly check-in |
| "What is this exactly?" | Curious — Qualification needed | Ask qualifying questions |
| "Who are you / how did you get this number?" | Defensive — Disarm first | Address directly, don't pitch |
| No reply | Cold — Continue DM sequence | Touch 2, 3, 4 |

### 2.5 — The Revenue Audit as a Nurture Weapon

For leads who want proof before committing to a call, offer the **Free After-Hours Revenue Audit** as described in `hormozi_gems_applied.md` Gem 3. This is a value-first tool that converts a "show me more" request into a 7-day engagement.

```
Here's something we've been doing for properties that want to see

We run a free 7-day After-Hours Revenue Audit for [Hotel Name].

Interested?
```

**Audit Delivery Process:**

2. Manually monitor their WhatsApp inquiry patterns for 5–7 days (or use the demo simulation if they can't share access — simulate based on property type and comparable data)
3. Compile a 1-page report: inquiry volume, estimated missed revenue, sample AI response demonstration
4. Send the report as a PDF via WhatsApp
5. Follow-up within 24 hours of sending: "Did you get a chance to look at the audit? What surprised you most?"

The audit converts at a dramatically higher rate than a cold call because the prospect has already received value. The report IS the pitch.

### 3.3 — Pre-Call Ritual (10 Minutes Before Every Demo)

Do this before every demo call. Every time. Without exception.

1. **Open their hotel's Booking.com / Agoda listing.** Confirm ADR (use the cheapest available room as a proxy). Note the exact room count. Screenshot if helpful.
2. **Run the revenue calculation** (formula in `sales_script_complete.md` Section 2). Write the final monthly RM number on a sticky note — you will open with this.
3. **Check TripAdvisor reviews** for any mention of slow WhatsApp/email response. If you find one, you can quote it during the call: *"I actually saw a review from [month] where a guest mentioned they waited until morning for a reply..."*
4. **Find their exact WhatsApp number.** Confirm it's active (send a test "." message and check for delivery ticks — do not send anything with text).
5. **Open the Nocturn AI demo environment.** Test it once yourself. Confirm the AI is responding correctly.
6. **Prepare the demo WhatsApp number** you'll give them to test during the call.
7. **Review your CRM notes** for this lead. What did they say in the DM conversation? What was their stated pain? Reference it on the call.

---

### 3.4 — Post-Call Protocol (Within 2 Hours of Every Call)

Whether they closed or not:

1. **Update CRM.** Move to correct stage. Add notes from the call (what they said, objections raised, what resonated).
2. **Send the follow-up message.** Use the templates from `sales_script_complete.md` Part 4 based on their outcome. Send within 2 hours — while you're still fresh in their memory.
3. **If they closed:** Immediately trigger the onboarding protocol (see Part 4). Send the welcome message + wizard link within 1 hour of verbal yes.
4. **If they asked for the one-pager:** Send it now. Include their specific RM number from the revenue calculation — not a generic template.
5. **Book the next touchpoint.** Either a follow-up call, or a calendar reminder to reach out in X days. Never leave a lead in limbo.
6. **Self-review using the call scorecard** (from `sales_script_complete.md` Part 8). What score did you give yourself? What to improve next call?

### 3.5 — Deal Velocity Targets

## PART 4 — ONBOARDING

### 4.1 — Why Onboarding is the Most Important Stage

This is where churn is created or prevented. The AI is only as good as the KB it runs on. A hotel that signs up and never properly fills the KB will see the AI hallucinating, giving wrong rates, or deflecting questions — and they will cancel.

The churn cascade:
```
Incomplete KB → Wrong AI answers → Staff still handling WhatsApp manually
  → GM thinks "this isn't working" → Cancels at day 30 → We lose the case study AND the referral
```

Preventing this is the entire point of onboarding. The goal is not just "AI live in 48 hours." The goal is: **AI live in 48 hours, answering correctly at least 80% of queries, with the GM seeing the value within the first week.**

### 5.1 — The Retention Philosophy

Retention is not a customer success function. Retention is a product + process function. If the AI is answering correctly and the GM sees revenue numbers every morning, they will not cancel. If either of those breaks, they will.

The retention process exists to:
1. Ensure those two things never break
2. Surface problems early enough to fix them
3. Convert satisfied clients into advocates and upsells

### 5.5 — Churn Prevention: Early Warning System

Check these signals weekly for every active client:

**Yellow Flags (address within 3 days):**
- No conversations in the last 5 days (AI may be disconnected)
- Client hasn't opened dashboard in 10+ days
- Escalation rate > 40% (KB gaps)
- Daily report missed 2+ consecutive days

**Red Flags (address within 24 hours):**
- Client sends a frustrated WhatsApp (wrong AI answer, complaint from a guest)
- Conversation volume drops >60% week-over-week
- Client says "I'm thinking of pausing this"

**Response to Red Flag:**
Call them. Do not WhatsApp. Do not email. Call.

Opening: "I saw [X signal] and wanted to call straight away. Tell me what's happening."

Listen completely before responding. Identify the root cause. Commit to a specific fix by a specific time. Follow up within 24 hours to confirm it's resolved.

The goal of the red flag call is not to save the account (that's the wrong frame). The goal is to fix the problem. If the problem is fixed, the account saves itself.
