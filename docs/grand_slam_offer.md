> **Grand Slam Offer Formula (Hormozi):**
> Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
>
> An offer is a Grand Slam when saying no feels **stupid** — not just risky.
> Every design decision in this document moves the numerator up or the denominator down.

## 2. Who This Is For — The ICP Filter

This offer is not for everyone. It is designed to be irresistible to one specific type of hotel operator. If the GM does not match at least 4 of 5 below, do not proceed.

| Filter | The Right Fit | Wrong Fit |
|--------|---------------|-----------|
| **Property type** | Independent or small-brand 3–4 star hotel | Budget hostel, Airbnb, 5-star chain with enterprise procurement |
| **Size** | 40–150 rooms | Under 40 (too few inquiries to prove ROI) or over 150 (chain procurement delays) |
| **Location** | Malaysia — Klang Valley, Penang, JB, Kota Kinabalu | Outside Malaysia in v1 |
| **After-hours gap** | Front desk closes at or before 10pm; no one actively answers WhatsApp after close | 24/7 staffed reservations |
| **Channel dependency** | WhatsApp is the primary booking inquiry channel (60%+ of manual inquiries) | Exclusively phone or OTA-dependent |
| **OTA pain** | Paying 15%+ commission on Agoda or Booking.com and feeling it | No OTA dependency or doesn't see it as a problem |
| **Decision maker** | GM, Revenue Manager, or property Owner who controls the budget | IT manager, marketing coordinator |

**The hair-on-fire test:** Does the GM have a dark window every night where they KNOW bookings are being lost and they can feel it? That visceral certainty — "we're missing money every night" — is the only qualifying condition that matters. Everything else is context.

## 3. The Problem, Precisely Stated

### What Happens Every Night at 10pm

The hotel's reservation desk closes. The team goes home. The WhatsApp Business account sits on someone's phone. Guests — mostly domestic Malaysian travellers booking for that weekend — are still awake and sending messages.

A guest sends:
> *"Hi ada bilik untuk this Saturday? Berapa harga untuk 2 malam?"*

No one answers.

The guest waits 5 minutes. Opens Agoda. Finds your hotel. Books through Agoda. You pay RM 207 in commission (18% on RM 230 ADR × 2 nights) on a guest who was already **trying to give you their money directly**.

This happens 8–25 times per night depending on your property size and season.

### The Three Compounding Losses

| Loss | What It Costs |
|------|--------------|
| **1. The booking itself** | Goes to OTA. Guest never returns to your direct channel. |
| **2. The OTA commission** | 15–18% on every booking they recover for you. RM 100–350 per booking at typical ADRs. |
| **3. The future relationship** | An OTA guest is Agoda's customer. A direct booking guest is yours. Repeat rate for direct bookings is 3× higher than OTA. |

**The GM's hidden loss:** Most GMs estimate they receive "a few messages" after hours. The real number is typically 3–5× their estimate. The data doesn't exist until someone installs a listener.

### 5.3 — The 3% Performance Fee

**The most important thing to understand about the 3%:**

It is not a commission on bookings you would have gotten anyway. It is exclusively a share of bookings we demonstrably facilitated — conversations that went through the Nocturn AI system, with a lead record created, and a conversion marked by staff.

**The comparison that makes the 3% feel like generosity:**

| Booking Channel | Commission / Cost |
|----------------|------------------|
| Agoda | 15–25% per booking |
| Booking.com | 15–25% per booking |
| Expedia | 15–22% per booking |
| Your own website (if they found it) | 3–5% payment processing only |
| **Nocturn AI** | **3% on confirmed facilitated bookings** |

**The real comparison is not Nocturn AI vs. nothing. It is Nocturn AI vs. the OTA that captures your dropped inquiries.**

When a guest messages your hotel at 11pm and gets no reply, they don't stop wanting a room. They open Agoda. Your hotel appears. They book. You pay 18%. Nocturn AI's 3% is the alternative to that 18% — applied only to the bookings where we did the work.

**The math at a typical property:**

| Scenario | Without Nocturn | With Nocturn |
|----------|----------------|--------------|
| After-hours inquiry arrives 11pm | No response | AI responds in 28 seconds |
| Guest experience | Frustrated, leaves | Answered, books direct |
| Booking path | Agoda → 18% commission | Direct → 3% Nocturn fee |
| On a RM 460 booking (2 nights × RM 230 ADR) | RM 83 net after RM 82 Agoda fee (18%) = RM 378 to hotel | RM 445 net after RM 14 Nocturn fee (3%) = RM 431 to hotel |
| **Difference per booking** | — | **+RM 53 per booking vs. OTA path** |

**But that math understates the real value.** The OTA scenario assumes the booking happens regardless. In reality, many guests who don't get a reply at 11pm simply abandon the enquiry — they book a different hotel, not your hotel through Agoda. The 3% is being charged on revenue that would otherwise be zero.

**Performance fee mechanics — the four protections:**

1. **Staff control:** The "Converted" marking and booking value are entered by hotel staff. Nocturn AI cannot self-report a conversion. You only pay 3% on what you've personally verified.
2. **Clear attribution:** A booking only qualifies if there is a corresponding conversation and lead record in the system. No conversation = no fee.
3. **Monthly calculation:** Calculated at month-end on the previous month's confirmed conversions. You see the data before the invoice.
4. **Exit at any time:** Cancel the service, 3% liability ends immediately. No tail commissions.

### Day -1: The First Call (15 Minutes)

SheersSoft calls within 2 hours of application submission.

**Purpose:** Confirm ICP fit, surface the pain in the GM's own words, calibrate the ROI estimate.

**The four questions that matter:**

1. *"How many WhatsApp inquiries would you estimate come in after your team goes home — just a rough sense?"* (Volume qualifier — and primes them for the data they don't have)
2. *"What happens to those right now?"* (Pain confirmation — let them say "nothing" in their own words)
3. *"What percentage of your bookings would you say currently go through OTA versus direct?"* (OTA dependency — surfaces the commission cost)
4. *"Who else, besides yourself, would need to see this working before you'd be comfortable continuing?"* (Stakeholder mapping — ensures no surprise blockers after Day 30)

**The outcome of this call:** GM either qualifies themselves out (rare) or agrees to receive a proposal and timeline. Do not demo on this call. The demo is after they've agreed to proceed, not a replacement for pre-qualification.

## 10. Objection Destruction

### "The 3% feels like another commission"

It is. But it's 3% versus the 15–18% you're currently paying OTAs on bookings that started as direct inquiries. The guest who messages you at 11pm and gets no reply doesn't disappear — they book through Agoda and you pay 6× more.

Additionally: the 3% only applies to bookings we demonstrably facilitated. You control the conversion marking. You enter the booking value. If we didn't play a role in the booking, you don't mark it — no fee.

---

### "What if the AI says something wrong?"

Two safeguards: (1) The AI only quotes what's in the knowledge base. If a rate or policy isn't there, it doesn't make one up — it says "Let me connect you with our team." (2) Any wrong answer reported to SheersSoft is corrected within 2 business hours during the 30-day onboarding period.

In 25+ hotel deployments of conversational AI in Southeast Asia, the most common AI error is not fabrication — it's omission (not having a rate or policy in the KB). Omission triggers a handoff. It doesn't create a booking conflict.

---

### "We already have a chatbot on our website"

Website chatbots handle approximately 5–15% of hotel inquiries. The remaining 85–95% come through WhatsApp, phone, and email. Your website chatbot is covering a small fraction of the problem. Nocturn AI covers the full problem — with a focus on WhatsApp, which is the dominant channel.

---

### "I need to check with IT"

There is no IT project. The web widget is one line of HTML that any webmaster can paste in 30 seconds. The WhatsApp integration requires a one-time login to your WhatsApp Business Manager — no server access, no PMS integration, no network changes. We have onboarded hotels in 48 hours without involving an IT team.

---

### "What if we cancel — are there exit fees?"

None. The only committed cost is the RM 999 setup fee, which has already been delivered. Cancel at any time — the monthly platform fee and performance fees stop immediately. No tail commissions. No notice period.

---

### "Can we try for free first?"

The product is not free because our team's time has real cost. What we offer instead is better than free: a **performance guarantee** — you only pay 3% on results, and if we don't deliver results in Month 1, your Month 2 is free.

The RM 999 setup is real work delivered upfront. It would cost RM 5,000–12,000 from any other vendor. The guarantee structure means your exposure is RM 999 + RM 199 — and if the first month produces even one confirmed booking, you've covered that cost.

### "Our guests prefer to speak to a human"

Every guest who says "I want to speak to a person" will be transferred to your team with full conversation context within seconds. The AI doesn't fight for guests who want human contact — it flags them as priority handoffs. What the AI handles is the 80% of inquiries that are factual: rates, availability, room types, facilities. Your team handles the 20% that require relationship and judgment.
