## 1. ICP Summary

**The ideal customer.** If a property does not match at least 4 of 5 criteria, deprioritise.

| Criterion | Qualifier | Disqualifier |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| **Geography** | Malaysia (primary: KL, Penang, JB, KK) | Outside Malaysia for v1 |
| **Star rating** | 3★ or 4★ | 1★, 2★, or 5★ luxury chains |
| **Room count** | 50–300 rooms | <30 (too small) or >400 (enterprise) |
| **Booking channels** | WhatsApp / email / phone as primary channels | Fully PMS-automated, no manual channel dependency |
| **Inquiry volume** | >20 inquiries/day | <10 inquiries/day |
| **After-hours gap** | Reservations closes at night or weekends | 24/7 staffed reservations team |
| **Ownership** | Independent or small regional chain | Global chain with mandated enterprise software |

**Who to talk to:**
- **Primary:** General Manager, Revenue Manager, Director of Reservations
- **Secondary:** Owner (for independent properties), Head of Sales
- **Not the right person:** IT Manager, Marketing Executive (they do not control reservations budget)

## 5. Stage 2 — Discovery Call (Question Bank)

**Goal:** Understand their pain well enough to give a demo that hits the exact nerve. Do not pitch until you have answered the 5 core questions below.

**Duration:** 15–30 minutes
**Format:** WhatsApp voice / Zoom / phone — their preference
**Opener:** *"Before I show you anything, I'd love to understand how [Hotel Name] handles reservations today — is that okay?"*

---

### Section A — Volume & Channel Mapping (understand the problem)

| # | Question | What you're listening for |
|---|----------|--------------------------|
| A1 | *"On a typical day, roughly how many inquiries come into the hotel across all channels — WhatsApp, email, phone, walk-in?"* | >20/day = strong ICP. Note the number for ROI calc. |
| A2 | *"Which channel is most active — WhatsApp, email, or phone calls?"* | WhatsApp dominant = perfect fit |
| A3 | *"What time does your reservations desk officially close in the evening?"* | If they say 6pm or earlier, the after-hours pain is confirmed |
| A4 | *"What happens to a WhatsApp inquiry that comes in at, say, 10pm on a weekday?"* | Listen for: "nothing until morning," "someone checks it from home," "the phone is shared" |
| A5 | *"On your busiest days — check-in, events, peak season — does your team manage to respond to every inquiry the same day?"* | Listen for: "we try," "sometimes things slip," "we have a backlog" |

---

### Section B — Revenue Leakage (surface the loss)

| # | Question | What you're listening for |
|---|----------|--------------------------|
| B1 | *"What percentage of your bookings would you say come through direct channels — WhatsApp, email, walk-in — versus OTA?"* | >50% direct = perfect fit; confirms manual channel dependency |
| B2 | *"Do you have a sense of how much commission you're paying OTAs monthly?"* | Let them say the number. It always hurts more when they say it. |
| B3 | *"Have you ever had a guest say they tried to reach you and couldn't get through?"* | Any "yes" here is gold — ask for the story |
| B4 | *"Does the GM receive any kind of morning report on overnight inquiries or leads?"* | "No" = easy pitch for the morning report feature |
| B5 | *"How do you currently track leads — CRM, WhatsApp, Excel, or something else?"* | Excel or "nothing" = massive pain; confirms no competitor present |

---

### Section C — Team & Operations (qualify the buyer)

| # | Question | What you're listening for |
|---|----------|--------------------------|
| C1 | *"How many people are on your reservations team?"* | 2–5 people = ideal; confirms staffing constraint |
| C2 | *"Who typically handles the reservations WhatsApp? Is it on one person's phone?"* | Single phone = huge pain point; mention it in the demo |
| C3 | *"Is the GM involved in day-to-day inquiry handling, or is it fully delegated?"* | If GM is involved, you want them on the demo call |
| C4 | *"What does a typical peak-season day look like for your reservations team?"* | Let them vent. This is your demo material. |

---

### Section D — Previous Attempts & Objections (pre-handle)

| # | Question | What you're listening for |
|---|----------|--------------------------|
| D1 | *"Have you tried any tools before — chatbots, CRMs, booking software — to handle this?"* | If yes: what failed and why → use this in demo |
| D2 | *"When you think about adding a tool like this, what's your biggest concern?"* | Price, complexity, guest experience, IT dependency — note for objection handling |
| D3 | *"Is this a decision you could make for the property, or does it need to go up to ownership?"* | Identify the real decision-maker before investing in demo |

---

### Discovery Call Closer

After the questions:

> *"Based on what you've told me — [X] inquiries a day, reservations closing at [time], [team situation] — this is exactly the problem we built Nocturn AI for. Let me show you a 20-minute demo with a property that sounds very similar to yours. Would [specific day and time] work for you?"*

**Do not proceed to demo without:**
- [ ] Confirmed inquiry volume (A1)
- [ ] Confirmed after-hours gap (A3, A4)
- [ ] Decision-maker identified (D3)
- [ ] Demo scheduled

## 6. Stage 3 — Demo Script

**Duration:** 20–30 minutes
**Attendees to request:** GM + Revenue or Reservations Manager (both)
**Setup:** Live product, screen share, real demo data loaded

**Before the call:**
- [ ] Load the dashboard with realistic demo data for their property size
- [ ] Know their ADR (look up Booking.com average rate for the property)
- [ ] Have the ROI estimate pre-calculated using: `daily_inquiries × 0.30 × ADR × 0.20 × 30`
- [ ] Prepare one WhatsApp conversation in English, one in BM/Manglish

---

### OPENING (2 minutes) — Anchor on their pain

> *"Before I show you the product, let me confirm I heard you correctly on our last call."*

Playback their discovery call answers:
> *"You mentioned you get around [X] inquiries a day, reservations closes at [time], and WhatsApp is sitting on [person]'s phone. And you said the biggest pain is [their exact words]. Does that still sound right?"*

Wait for confirmation. Then:
> *"Everything I'm going to show you in the next 20 minutes is designed specifically for that situation. Let me start with the one number your GM cares about."*

---

### ACT 1 — THE MONEY SLIDE (3 minutes)

*Open the dashboard home.*

> *"This is the first thing your GM sees every morning. Not a setup checklist — not a feature menu. Just: how many inquiries came in, how many leads were captured, and how much revenue was recovered while the team was offline."*

Point to the revenue card:
> *"This number uses a conservative formula — your ADR, times the number of high-intent leads, times a 20% conversion rate. For a property like yours, getting [X] after-hours inquiries a night, that's roughly **RM [calculated amount]/month** sitting in your WhatsApp that nobody is picking up."*

Pause. Let it land.

> *"Now let me show you where that number comes from."*

---

### ACT 2 — THE 11PM TEST (5 minutes)

*Switch to the WhatsApp demo view.*

> *"I'm going to be your guest. It's 11pm. Your team has gone home."*

**Send in English:**
> *"Hi, do you have rooms available this Friday? How much for a standard room?"*

Let the AI respond. Point to response time.
> *"[Response time] seconds. No staff involved. The guest has an answer before they even consider opening Agoda."*

**Switch to BM/Manglish:**
> *"Jom, I try in Bahasa Malaysia — the way a real Malaysian guest would type it."*

Send:
> *"Hai, ada bilik tak this weekend? Berapa harga dia?"*

Let it respond in BM.
> *"It didn't break. It understood the question, responded in their language, and asked a qualifying question. Your guest feels heard. Your team doesn't have to wake up at 11pm."*

**Send the group booking trigger:**
> *"What if I told you I'm looking for 20 rooms for 3 nights for a wedding?"*

Show it escalating, flagging as high-value.
> *"It flagged this as a high-value lead before your team even knew the guest existed."*

---

### ACT 3 — THE LEADS DASHBOARD (4 minutes)

*Navigate to Leads.*

> *"While we were having that conversation, the AI was building this."*

Show the captured lead:
> *"Name — captured. Phone — captured. Channel — WhatsApp. Intent — group booking. Estimated value — [RM X]. Status — queued for morning follow-up."*

> *"Your sales team doesn't start the day reading 50 WhatsApp messages. They open this, and they know exactly who to call first and why."*

Show the status filters:
> *"Everything is tracked through the lifecycle. New → Contacted → Qualified → Converted. And when a lead doesn't convert — you can mark it Lost and record why. That's your data for understanding where bookings are leaking."*

---

### ACT 4 — THE HANDOFF (3 minutes)

*Navigate to Conversations → filter by Handed Off.*

> *"Not every guest wants to talk to an AI. Some have complaints. Some have complex requests. Some just say 'I want to speak to a person.'"*

Show the handoff status:
> *"The moment the AI detects a complaint or a request it can't handle — it stops replying and flags the conversation for your team. Staff open this, they see the full context — every message, the AI's responses, the guest's sentiment. They don't ask the guest to repeat themselves."*

Show the staff reply input:
> *"And they reply from right here. The guest gets your message instantly on WhatsApp. The whole conversation is in one place."*

---

### ACT 5 — THE MORNING REPORT (2 minutes)

> *"Every morning, your GM receives this email."*

Show the daily report format:
> *"Inquiries handled overnight. Leads captured. Channels active. Estimated revenue recovered. One CTA to open the dashboard. It takes 45 seconds to read and answers the question every GM asks: 'What did we miss last night?' The answer is: nothing."*

---

### CLOSING (5 minutes)

> *"Let me put a number on your situation specifically."*

Open a blank calculator or share your pre-calculated estimate:

> *"Based on [X] daily inquiries, [ADR], and a conservative 20% conversion rate on after-hours leads — you have approximately **RM [amount]/month** in revenue at risk from unanswered inquiries. That's not counting what you're paying Booking.com to capture the leads you're dropping."*

> *"We charge RM 199 a month and 3% only on direct bookings we actually close for you. Compare that to what you're paying Booking.com — 15–18% on every booking, including guests who were already trying to reach you directly. Setup is a one-time RM 999. We handle everything — connect your WhatsApp number, drop a script tag on your website, configure your email. Your team spends 30 minutes with me sharing your rates, room types, and FAQs. You're live in 48 hours."*

> *"And here's our guarantee: if in 30 days we haven't demonstrated revenue recovery, we waive the following month's platform fee. We only win when you win."*

**Close question:**
> *"Does RM 199 a month plus 3% on bookings we recover make sense for [Hotel Name]? I can have you live by [specific date]."*

---

### "We need to check with the owner / board."

**Response:**
> *"Absolutely — I'd actually like to be part of that conversation if you're open to it. I can prepare a one-page ROI summary with [Hotel Name]'s actual pilot numbers and walk the owner through the math. It usually takes 10 minutes."*

> *"Would it help if I prepared a short deck for you to share? I can have it ready today."*

---

### "We tried a chatbot before and it didn't work."

**Response:**
> *"What failed about it?"*

[Let them answer. Listen for: wrong channel, sounded robotic, guests ignored it, IT dependency, didn't capture leads.]

> *"That's exactly why we built this differently. [Address their specific failure mode.] Did the previous chatbot work on WhatsApp? That's where 90% of Malaysian hotel inquiries come from — most chatbots only run on the website."*

---

### "Our guests prefer talking to a real person."

**Response:**
> *"They do — and so do we. Nocturn AI's job is not to replace your team. It's to make sure the guest gets a response at 11pm when your team is at home, so they don't book on Agoda before your team can talk to them in the morning. The AI's job ends the moment the guest is ready to be handed off to a human — and it passes the full conversation context to your team immediately."*

---

### "We're already using [PMS / Opera / specific software]."

**Response:**
> *"Good — we don't replace it and don't need to integrate with it. Nocturn AI sits completely outside your PMS. It captures inquiries on WhatsApp and email and hands your team a qualified lead with all the guest details. Your team then takes over and completes the booking in whatever system you use. We're the net before the PMS, not inside it."*

---

### "What about PDPA? Our guests' data..."

**Response:**
> *"We built PDPA compliance in from day one. Guest data is encrypted at rest and in transit. Each property's data is fully isolated — we can't see another hotel's guests, and neither can they. We comply with PDPA 2010 and publish our privacy policy. I can share the technical documentation if you need it for your compliance review."*

---

### "Can we extend the pilot?"

**Response:**
> *"The pilot period is 30 days — it's designed to give you enough data to make a real decision. If after 30 days you feel you need more time, I'm open to a short extension on the condition that we review the numbers together and I understand what's still unclear. What would make the decision easier for you?"*
