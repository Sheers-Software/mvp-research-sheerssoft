# Nocturn AI — Complete Sales Script
## Cold WhatsApp DM → Qualification → Demo Call → Close

> **Ground truth going in:** You have zero paying clients. The product is built and works. Your weapon is the guarantee — it eliminates risk so completely that saying no is irrational. Internalize this: you are not asking anyone to trust you. You are asking them to try something they literally cannot lose money on.

---

## THE SALES ARCHITECTURE

```
Cold DM (WhatsApp)
  → Get a response (goal: one reply)
    → Qualify in 2–3 messages (goal: confirm they have the problem)
      → Book a 20-min call (goal: get them on Zoom/Teams)
        → Demo Call — CLOSER Framework (goal: close or handle objections)
          → Close on call OR follow-up sequence
            → Onboarding begins
```

The entire process should take no more than 5–7 days from first DM to signed agreement. If it drags past 7 days without a clear "yes" or "no", you have a ghost and need to re-qualify or move on.

## PART 1 — COLD DM (WhatsApp)

### The One Rule
The DM has one job: **get a reply.** Not sell. Not explain. Not pitch. One reply.

Anything that sounds like a sales message gets deleted in 3 seconds. Hotel GMs are approached by vendors constantly. You need to sound like a person who noticed something specific about their hotel — not a salesperson with a script.

### Pre-DM Research (5 minutes per hotel)
Before sending, find:
- Hotel name and number of rooms (TripAdvisor / Booking.com)
- Whether they have an active WhatsApp number listed (their website or Google Maps)
- Rough ADR (from Booking.com / Agoda listing)
- Whether they have overnight/24-hour staff (read a few reviews mentioning "late check-in" or "after-hours")

This takes 5 minutes. It's what separates a 3% reply rate from a 15% reply rate.

---

### Opener A — The Specific Question (Best for most GMs)

```
Hi [Name / "Team"],

Quick question — does [Hotel Name] have someone monitoring your WhatsApp
after 10pm, or do guests have to wait until morning for a reply?

Asking because I work with hotels in Klang Valley and noticed something
interesting about after-hours inquiry patterns.
```

**Why this works:**
- Hyper-specific — implies you know their situation
- Ends with a hook ("noticed something interesting") that creates curiosity without revealing the pitch
- It's a yes/no question — low effort to answer
- Not salesy — reads like a researcher, not a vendor

---

### Opener B — The Number Hook (Best for data-oriented GMs)

```
Hi [Name],

Random but — most 3-star hotels in KL lose between RM 8,000–15,000/month
from WhatsApp inquiries that come in after reception closes.

Is this something [Hotel Name] has looked at before?
```

**Why this works:**
- Leads with a specific, credible number (not a vague claim)
- Creates urgency without being pushy
- The question invites them to say "no we haven't" — which IS qualification

---

### Opener C — The Observation (Best for hotels with obvious WhatsApp presence)

```
Hi, saw [Hotel Name] has WhatsApp on your website — do you handle
booking inquiries through it?

(Reason I'm asking comes after your reply)
```

**Why this works:**
- Shortest possible opener — lowest friction
- The "(Reason I'm asking comes after your reply)" is a pattern interrupt that almost always generates curiosity replies
- Never gets blocked as spam — reads completely human

---

### Opener D — Direct (Best for boutique hotels with owner-GMs)

```
Hi [Name], I'm [Your Name] from SheersSoft.

We built an AI concierge that handles hotel WhatsApp inquiries overnight
and captures booking leads while your team sleeps.

One question — how many messages does [Hotel Name] typically get
after 10pm on a weekday?
```

Use this only when: (a) you have a referral or warm intro, or (b) the GM's profile suggests they're direct/no-nonsense. This opener is more salesy but converts faster with the right person.

---

### The Qualification Exchange (3–5 messages after opener)

Once they reply, your goal is to answer three questions before booking a call:
1. Do they actually have the after-hours inquiry problem? (Volume)
2. Are they the decision maker?
3. Are they open to a solution?

**If they say "Yes, we do have someone":**
```
Got it — so you have overnight front desk coverage? How do they handle WhatsApp
specifically — do they reply from the hotel's number or their personal phone?
```
(Probe for the real gap — "overnight front desk" often means security guard who ignores WhatsApp.)

**If they say "No, we don't have overnight coverage" or "Guests wait till morning":**
```
That's actually pretty common — most hotels I've spoken with are in the same position.

At your size, that probably means anywhere from 5 to 20+ inquiries per week
are either going unanswered or getting delayed responses. Some of those are
guests who've already booked with a competitor by morning.

Would it be worth a 20-minute call this week to see exactly how much
revenue this is costing [Hotel Name]? I can pull up the numbers for you.
```

**If they ask "What is this about?" or "What do you do?":**
```
We built an AI that links to your hotel's WhatsApp via a 5-minute QR scan, reads guest inquiries overnight, and drafts perfect replies in a dashboard for your staff to approve the next morning—complete with payment links.

It's live in 48 hours. No setup fee for the 7-day Shadow Pilot. You don't pay a cent unless you see the verified revenue proof.

The 20-min call is just to run your numbers — so you can see if the ROI
makes sense for [Hotel Name] specifically.
```

**If they are NOT the decision maker:**
```
Makes sense — who would be the right person to loop in for a conversation
like this? Even just a 20-min call to review the numbers?
```
(Get a name. Then ask: "Is it okay if I mention your name when I reach out to them?")

---

### Booking the Call

```
Are you free for 20 minutes this [Tuesday/Wednesday] afternoon?
Either 2pm or 4pm works on my end — whichever is easier for you.

I'll show you the actual AI demo and we can calculate exactly what
after-hours inquiries are costing [Hotel Name] right now.
```

**Critical:** Offer two specific time slots. Never say "whenever you're free" — it creates decision fatigue and they'll deprioritize it. Two slots force a micro-commitment.

If they don't confirm within 24 hours:
```
Just following up on this — still have both [Tuesday 2pm] and
[Wednesday 4pm] open. Which works better?
```

If no reply to follow-up within 48 hours:
```
No worries if the timing isn't right. I'll reach back out in a couple
of weeks — meanwhile, if you ever want to run the numbers for [Hotel Name],
just reply here and I'll set it up same day.
```
(Leave the door open. Never burn a lead.)

## PART 2 — PRE-CALL PREPARATION

Do this before every call. Takes 10 minutes. Determines 50% of your close rate.

### Research Checklist

| Item | Where to find | Why you need it |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| Exact room count | Booking.com / Agoda listing | For revenue calculation |
| ADR (average daily rate) | Booking.com (check lowest available) | For revenue calculation |
| Occupancy estimate | TripAdvisor reviews + gut feel | For recovery potential |
| WhatsApp number | Hotel website / Google Maps | Confirm it's active |
| GM's name | Hotel website / LinkedIn | Personalization |
| Reviews mentioning slow responses | TripAdvisor / Google | Pain evidence you can quote |

### Pre-Call Revenue Calculation

Before you join the call, calculate their after-hours loss estimate:

```
After-hours inquiry estimate: rooms × 0.15 per week (conservative)
  → Example: 80-room hotel = ~12 after-hours inquiries/week

Conversion rate assumption: 30% of unresponded inquiries = lost bookings
  → 12 × 30% = ~3.6 bookings lost per week

Revenue lost per week: 3.6 × ADR × avg stay (1.8 nights)
  → Example: 3.6 × RM 280 × 1.8 = ~RM 1,814/week

Monthly revenue lost: RM 1,814 × 4.3 = ~RM 7,800/month
```

Write this number down. You will open the demo call with it.

### Demo Setup

Have ready before the call:
- Nocturn AI demo environment open in a browser tab
- The actual WhatsApp demo number ready to send a test message to
- A sample daily revenue report screenshot
- The pricing one-pager (or the Grand Slam Offer text from memory)
- Their hotel name, room count, and ADR written on a sticky note

## PART 3 — THE DEMO CALL (CLOSER FRAMEWORK)

**Target duration:** 20–35 minutes. Never go over 45.
**Platform:** Zoom or Teams with screen share.
**Tone:** Consultant, not salesperson. You are here to diagnose their situation and show them the math.

---

### OPENING (2 minutes)

```
"[Name], appreciate you making the time. Quick agenda for us:

I'm going to ask you a few questions about how [Hotel Name] handles
after-hours inquiries today — takes about 5 minutes.

Then I'll show you the AI demo live — you can actually message it yourself
right now and see what your guests would experience.

And then we'll run the numbers together — see what the recovery opportunity
looks like for your specific property.

Total should be under 25 minutes. Sound good?"
```

**Why this opening works:**
- Sets expectations (removes anxiety about a long sales call)
- The agenda is theirs ("we'll run YOUR numbers") not yours
- Ends with a micro-commitment ("sound good?") — they're now engaged

---

### C — CLARIFY (5 minutes)
*Goal: Understand their current situation. Get them to describe the problem in their own words.*

```
"Let me start with a quick question — today, when a guest sends a WhatsApp
to [Hotel Name] after your front desk closes, what actually happens?"
```

[Let them answer fully. Do not interrupt.]

**Follow-up probes (use 2–3, don't run through all of them):**

```
"And roughly what time does the last person who monitors WhatsApp finish for the day?"

"On a busy week — say school holidays or long weekends — how many messages
would you estimate come in after that cutoff?"

"Has there been a case where a guest sent a message overnight and ended up
booking somewhere else by morning — that you know of?"

"What's the current process for following up on those missed messages the next day?"

"Does your GM get any visibility into how many inquiries came in overnight,
or is it mostly unknown?"
```

**What you're listening for:**
- Volume of after-hours messages (even their guess is useful)
- Whether they know how many leads they lose (most don't — they'll say "I'm not sure")
- Emotional frustration signals ("it's a real problem", "we've been meaning to fix this")
- Who is responsible (GM, owner, front desk manager?)

**Close the Clarify phase:**
```
"Okay — and in an ideal world, what would you want to happen with those
overnight messages?"
```
[This gets them to verbalize the dream outcome in their own words — which you then reflect back in the S phase.]

---

### L — LABEL (2 minutes)
*Goal: Name their problem clearly. Show you understand it. Build trust.*

```
"So if I'm hearing you right — [Hotel Name] is receiving somewhere in the
range of [X–Y] WhatsApp messages per week after your team clocks off,
and right now those guests are either getting no reply until morning,
or a delayed reply that might be too late.

And because there's no visibility into what comes in overnight, your GM
doesn't really know what's being missed — it's a blind spot."
```

[Pause. Let them confirm or correct you.]

They will usually say "Yes, exactly" or "Yeah, it's a problem." This confirmation is gold — they've now identified themselves as someone with this problem.

If they correct you: adjust, re-label, and re-confirm. Getting the label right matters more than moving fast.

```
"That's actually one of the most common things I hear from properties your size.
The front desk team works hard during the day, but there's genuinely no way
for a human to monitor WhatsApp at 2am — that's not a staffing failure,
it's just a structural gap."
```

[This removes shame/defensiveness. They're now more open.]

---

### O — OVERVIEW PAST PAIN (3 minutes)
*Goal: Surface what they've already tried (or considered). Deepen emotional investment in finding a solution.*

```
"Has [Hotel Name] looked at any solutions for this before?

Like — auto-responders, hiring part-time staff for evenings,
or anything like that?"
```

**Common answers and how to respond:**

**"We have an auto-reply that says we'll get back to them in the morning"**
```
"How well does that convert? In my experience, a generic auto-reply
actually makes things worse — it confirms to the guest that no one is
there, so they immediately open Booking.com and book someone else.
Has that been your sense too?"
```

**"We've tried having the night security monitor it but they're not trained"**
```
"Exactly — and even if they were trained, you'd need them replying in
multiple languages, knowing rates, policies, availability. That's a
full-time front desk job. So what ended up happening — did the security
just stop monitoring it?"
```

**"Nothing — we haven't really done anything about it"**
```
"That's honest — and honestly it makes sense because up until recently
the only options were hire more staff or buy expensive enterprise hotel
software. There wasn't really a middle option."
```

**"We looked at some chatbot tools but they seemed complicated"**
```
"What made them feel complicated? Was it the setup, the ongoing
management, or the cost?"
```
[Whatever they say — "setup" or "cost" — you now know exactly how to position the 48-hour live / 30-minute wizard / RM 500 price point. Use it later.]

**Close the Overview phase:**
```
"So at this point — if there was a way to have every overnight inquiry
answered automatically, leads captured, and a report on the GM's desk
every morning... what would that change for [Hotel Name]?"
```

[This is a pivotal question. The answer becomes the foundation of your close.]

Third — and this is the most important one — you literally cannot
lose money on this.

We're not asking you to bet on us. You pay nothing for the first

## PART 4 — POST-CALL FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE

## PART 7 — MENTAL FRAMEWORKS (Read before every call)

### The Guarantee Is Your Superpower
You have no case studies. You have no testimonials. You have the most powerful sales tool available to a new SaaS company: a guarantee that makes saying "no" genuinely irrational.

The prospect doesn't need to trust you. They don't need to believe your product works. They just need to believe that if it doesn't work, they won't lose money. That is all. Lead with this when confidence wavers.

### You Are Not Asking for a Favour
You are offering a hotel GM a no-risk opportunity to recover revenue they are currently losing. You are doing them a favour by reaching out. The moment you feel like you're inconveniencing someone by following up — reread this line.

### The Numbers Are Your Anchor
When a call goes quiet or the prospect seems unconvinced, return to the numbers. Not features. Not benefits. Numbers. "We calculated RM [X]/month. Is that number still accurate to you?" Numbers are objective. It's hard to argue with math.

### One Objection = One Invitation
When a prospect raises an objection, they are telling you the last thing standing between them and a yes. Treat it as a gift. "Anything else?" after handling an objection signals you're not running — you're genuinely trying to resolve every concern. Do this until they've raised everything.

### Silence Is Power
After the logical close, after "is there any scenario where you lose?", after any direct question — stop talking. The first person to speak after a direct question loses. Let them answer. Silence is pressure without aggression.

### The Founding Client Reframe
"No clients yet" is only a weakness if you treat it as one. Reframed, it's: "You get founding access — direct contact with the builder, ability to shape the product, and a company that needs this to work as badly as you do." Every tech founder who ever landed their first enterprise client did it by making someone feel special for being first.
