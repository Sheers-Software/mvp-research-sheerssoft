## Executive Summary

v0.5 introduced two things simultaneously:

1. **The After-Hours Revenue Audit** (built and shipped) — a public calculator
   at `/audit` that gives hotel GMs an instant RM leakage estimate, captures
   their email, and feeds a lead pipeline in the admin panel.

2. **The Three-Stage Funnel** (architectural commitment) — a formal restatement
   of how the product acquires, converts, and retains clients:
   - Stage 1: Audit (free calculator, instant value, no commitment)
   - Stage 2: Shadow Pilot (7-day Twilio listener, real inquiry data)
   - Stage 3: Full Product (Meta Cloud API, AI responds, subscription)

The critical insight that drove the funnel redesign: WhatsApp has no passive
listener mode. A number is routed to either the phone app or the API webhook —
not both simultaneously. The shadow pilot sidesteps this by using a new Twilio
number the hotel promotes as a temporary "booking enquiries" channel. After 7
days of real data, the GM has proof. The number transfer objection evaporates.

The remaining Sprint 2.5 work (audit_only_mode flag, weekly email, provisioning)
is what completes the funnel and enables the first shadow pilot to run.

### PRD — Changes (v2.2 → v2.3)

#### Section 1 — Problem Statement
**Added:** Paragraph describing the "blind spot" — GMs don't know their actual after-hours volume until someone installs a listener. The shadow pilot solves this.
**Rationale (Gem 3):** The lead magnet works because it makes the invisible visible. "You're losing X per night" is compelling only when X is based on their data.

#### Section 2 — Design Principles
**Added:** Principle 7: "Proof Before Commitment" — never ask for WhatsApp API credentials before the GM has seen real data from their own number.
**Rationale (Gem 2):** The old model asked GMs to trust on faith. The shadow pilot gives proof before commitment, which directly addresses the primary objection to Meta Cloud API registration.

#### Section 3 — Target ICP
**Updated:** Size narrowed from 50–300 to 40–150 rooms with rationale.
**Updated:** Location narrowed from "Malaysia" to "Klang Valley/Penang/JB (initial market)" with density rationale.
**Added:** "Dark Window" qualifier (closes at 10pm or earlier) — the product's entire value proposition is in the dark window. Properties without one are not the ICP.
**Added:** "Channel Dependency" qualifier (WhatsApp as primary booking inquiry channel >60%) — validates the primary channel assumption.
**Added:** "OTA Dependency" qualifier (paying 15%+ Agoda commission) — the OTA displacement figure in the audit hits harder when the GM is already paying 18%.
**Updated:** Budget from RM 1,500–5,000 to RM 499–2,800 (reflecting actual Boutique tier pricing).
**Rationale (Gem 1):** Hormozi explicitly says narrow the niche until you can name your best 100 customers from memory. The new qualifiers make the ICP precise enough that the shadow pilot's inquiry volume will reliably impress the GM.

#### Section 4.1 — Feature Requirements
**Updated F2:** Added `audit_only_mode` documentation — when True, messages logged but AI does not respond. This is the shadow pilot's core behaviour.
**Added F9:** After-Hours Revenue Audit Calculator (already built) — public page, 5 inputs, instant output, lead gate.
**Added F10:** Shadow Pilot Mode — full specification including the WhatsApp architectural constraint, audit_only_mode flag, 7-day audit email, and transition to full product.
**Added F11:** Audit-to-Full Conversion Automation — SheersSoft AM notifications on audit submission and Day 7, automated sequences.
**Rationale (Gem 3 + Gem 4):** These are the three features that complete the lead magnet and speed-to-lead mechanics. Without F10 and F11, the audit calculator is a dead end — it captures leads but doesn't convert them.

#### Section 4.2 — Explicitly NOT in v1
**Added:** "Automated PDF report generation" — the audit email is text-based; designed PDF is post-PMF.

#### Section 5 — Success Metrics
**Added Product KPIs:** Audit → Shadow Pilot Conversion (>30%), Shadow Pilot → Full Product Conversion (>50%), Days Audit → Paid (<21 days).
**Rationale:** Without these funnel metrics, the audit calculator is a vanity feature. These metrics enforce that the funnel is actually converting.

#### Section 6 — Pricing
**Completely rewritten.** Old model had Starter/Professional/Enterprise tiers without a free entry stage. New model reflects the three-stage funnel:
- Stage 1 (Audit): Free
- Stage 2 (Shadow Pilot): Free, 7 days
- Stage 3 (Boutique/Independent/Premium): RM 499–2,800/mo
**Rationale (Gem 7 + Gem 12):** The Grand Slam Offer only works if the entry cost is zero. Every stage of the funnel has zero risk until the client has seen their own data.

#### Section 7 — Risks
**Added:** "GM refuses Meta Cloud API registration" risk with shadow pilot data as mitigation.
**Added:** "Shadow pilot volume too low" risk with pre-qualification via audit calculator as mitigation.

#### Section 8 — Go-To-Market
**Completely rewritten launch sequence.** Old sequence: pilot property → referrals → demos. New sequence: audit outreach → shadow pilot → full product → referral.
**Updated 60-second pitch** to reflect the "prove it first" framing.
**Rationale (Gem 1 + Gem 4):** The channel is still WhatsApp but the first message now delivers value (audit link) instead of asking for time (demo request).

## Decision Record: Why Shadow Pilot Instead of Full Pilot

The natural question is: why not just go straight to a full pilot with the real WhatsApp number?

Three reasons:

**1. The Meta Cloud API registration takes 2–4 weeks.** Even if the GM agrees today, they cannot transfer their WhatsApp Business number immediately. The shadow pilot fills this window productively — the GM sees data while Meta is processing their application.

**2. The KB cannot be built without knowing what questions guests ask.** The `/welcome` wizard asks GMs to enter their KB. Most GMs write what they THINK guests ask. Guests actually ask different things. The shadow pilot produces an exact question log. KB built from this log has materially higher AI accuracy on Day 1.

**3. The GM's objection is "I don't believe this will work for MY hotel."** No demo, no case study, and no industry statistic answers this objection. Only their own data does. The shadow pilot turns this objection into an asset: the GM's disbelief is exactly why they should do the 7-day test.
